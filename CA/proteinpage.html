Quinoa Stuffed Aubergine
Keeps for 7 days.
Cost:€7

Ingredients: 2 large aubergines, 12 Cherry Tomatoes, 85g Cup of Quinoa ,250ml of Stock,
1 Red Onion, 2 Cloves of Garlic, 35g Cup of Pine Nuts, 1 Teaspoon of Dried Italian Herbs,
2 Large Handfuls of Spinach, Handful of Fresh Basil, Olive Oil, Salt & Pepper.

Prep:
  1. Finely chop basil
  2. Half aubergines and cut out aubergine in cubes.
  3. Chop onion and garlic.
Method:
  1. Add aubergine shells, cubes and tomatoes to baking dish. Drizzle with olive oil, salt and pepper.
    Roast at 200͐ for 20 minutes.
  2. Add quinoa and stock to a small pot on medium heat. Cook for 10 minutes with the lid on.
  3. Set quinoa aside to steam.
  4. Heat olive oil on medium heat and add onions, garlic, pine nuts and dried herbs.
  5. Add cubed aubergine and tomatoes to pan. Keep shells in oven for another 10 minutes.
  6. Add basil, spinach and quinoa to the pan.
  7. Mix all ingredients together then scoop into aubergine shells.

Brown rice with chicken.
Keeps for 4 days.
Cost:€9

Ingredients:3 low sodium veg stock cube, 1 yellow pepper, 2 cloves of garlic, 2 chicken breasts,
1 cup of brown long grain rice, 1 head of broccoli, fresh thyme

Prep:
  1. Cut and deseed pepper.
  2. Chop garlic.
  3. Dice chicken breasts.
  4. Remove stalk from broccoli and cut.
  5. Finely chop thyme.
Method:
  1. Heat pot of water on medium heat, add onion and garlic. Cook for 6 minutes.
  2. Stir in chicken, rice and stock cubes.
  3. Lower the heat and allow to simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Add broccoli and cook for 15 minutes.
  5. Add pine nuts and thyme to serve.

Fiji style duck
Keeps for 3 days.
Cost:€11

Ingredients: 2 duck breasts, 1 white onion, garlic-ginger paste, ¼ tsp cumin spice, 2 sun dried chillies, 2 fresh red chillies, ¼ tsp chilli powder, 1 tbsp tumeric, ¼ cup of veg oil, ½ lime

Prep:
  1. Chop fresh chillies.
  2. Chop duck breast
  3. Dice white onion.
Method:
  1. Heat oil on high heat.
  2. Add dried chillies, cumin and fresh chillies. Allow to cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Add onion and salt and cook.
  4. Ad tumeric powder and garlic and stir.
  5. Add duck.
  6. Squeeze half a lime and add salt to taste.
  7. Lower heat to medium and allow to simmer until tender.
  8. Serve with rice.
